Discuss: Data Research, Project Intent

Less obvious perks of using 'live' data resources include logins and payments. How could Twitter be used in these two scenarios?

There are a lot of perks for using Twitter or Gmail to login to different sites. The number one most obvious one is a lot of time people don’t like having so many different usernames and passwords, so it makes it easier for the user to use what they are already familiar with to be a part of whatever app or site they are visiting. Another perk is giving them a sense of security knowing they are securely logging in. Another less obvious perk is with Twitter, it also integrates with the user's Twitter account, enabling them to share more easily, and effectively advertises for your app or site.

Visit a video service on the web (like YouTube) and search for "Screen Reader Demo" and "Mobile Screen Reader" - what are some of the common frustrations (or common support mechanisms) that you see among the examples that are given - how could you fix them (or use them)?

I’m not impaired in any way other than I don't like to read much, and I often times use the text to speech function of my macbook to read articles and even our assignments to me from time to time. Though often times it gets pretty annoying because it doesn't respect punctuation or times goes faster than you can comprehend that robotic voices; however, when I’m on my android device and use applications like Endomondo, and Google Now, that use the Google text to speech engine it is a much more enjoyable experience. It think implement ing a better speech engine would be a huge step in the right direction.

Other issues I saw with the demo’s were that often times the sites were not very friendly and would often just say link, link, link, and image not explaining what the link was for now what the image was of. That effectively makes the software completely useless, because who knows what you are looking at let alone cracking too. I don’t know that there is a way of fixing the screen reading software...what really needs to happen is the developers of these sites need to be fixed.

Devices: Please list two devices (and some of their features) that are made specifically to support Accessibility.

I haven't used iOS much (or really at all till I received my iPad) and don’t know how accessibility works in iOS but I'm sure there are features built in that make them work fairly well for the visually impaired. I do know Android pretty well though and have messed around with the talkback application, and being that they have such an advance text to speech engine now, and how well it integrates throughout the ui is very well. I even enabled talkback for a while just to see how it worked throughout different parts of the ui. Furthermore there are a lot of apps in the market that are designed to make the phone more usable.

As I mentioned earlier with my macbook, using the speech function in the control + click menu is very useful to easily assist visually impaired, not so much for the blink. I have also enabled access for assistive devices on my computer, and it call out the time to me on the hour and lets me know when applications need my attention.
